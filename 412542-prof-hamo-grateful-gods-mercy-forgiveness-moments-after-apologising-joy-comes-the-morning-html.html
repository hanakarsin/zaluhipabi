Churchill Show comedian Professor Hamo has expressed gratitude to God hours after publicly apologising to baby mama Jemutai and his children.

Via a social media post on May 19, Hamo quoted a Bible verse addressing God’s mercy and forgiveness.

“For his anger lasts only a moment, but his favour lasts a lifetime; weeping may stay for the night, but rejoicing comes in the morning,” he quoted the Book of Psalm.

It seems the funnyman finally has some peace after working things out with his wife Zippy and comedienne Jemutai.

Hamo’s message on forgiveness comes just a day after he admitted to being on the wrong over the paternity saga.

Jemutai, famous for starting her shows by saying “jibambe (have fun)”, had said she could not fend for their two children since he had neglected them.

Hamo divulged that he is patching up the relationship with his family.

He also expressed gratitude to his boss at the Churchill Show, Daniel Ndambuki and preacher Robert Burale for advising him on how to handle the matter best.

“To my young ones, life is not an easy road, papa is very sorry, and he hopes one day when you are old enough to understand you will forgive him,” he wrote to his children while vowing to always be there for them.

The Nakuru native apologised to his fans for letting them down and expressed optimism he will be more responsible.

“I am not yet there but working towards being a responsible person, dad, husband and man,” he posted.

Help

Jemutai, on the other hand, received assistance from a youth politician on May 19.

Moses Mwenda, the Mount Kenya Youth Alliance chairperson, discussed several business ideas with the comedienne.

“We have discussed several ideas on business venture going forward. Thank you, mheshimiwa, for your kindness and support,” Jemutai wrote.
