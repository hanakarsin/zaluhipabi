Saudi Arabia: Labour CS Simon Chelugui to Fly to Middle East amid Kenyan Workers' Complaints

June 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Labour CS Simon Chelugui said the government was concerned about reports of Kenyan migrant workers being mistreated by their Saudi employers
  • Chelugui urged job seekers to take personal responsibility and ensure they have seen the contract of employment before leaving Kenya
  • The CS also told the 420 recruitment agencies to adhere to the licensing requirements by the vetting board

Labour Cabinet Secretary (CS) Simon Chelugui is set to travel to Saudi Arabia to address issues of mistreatment, detention and torture of Kenyan migrant workers in Middle East countries.

Chelugui said the Ministry of Labour was working with the Ministry of Foreign affairs to address some of the concerns raised by Kenyans working in the Gulf.

“We are now in the final stages. These are part of activities I intend to finalise when I visit Saudi Arabia, possibly within the next 30 days,” Chelugui said.

Kenyans mistreated by employers in Saudi Arabia

The CS said the government was concerned about reports of Kenyan migrant workers being mistreated by their Saudi employers, forcing them to flee their homes, only to end up in detention centres.

"One of the objectives of this trip is to engage deeper in the protection of our migrant workers. We need assurance on payment of some basic welfare issues like leave and flexibility of the contracts so that in the event of any challenge between employee and employer, the employee is allowed to change employer," the CS said.

He urged job seekers to take personal responsibility and ensure they see the employment contract and understood it before boarding the plane to those countries.

"These agencies are expected to prepare for you contracts before you leave this country, so you will know who is yore employer where are they located, so that as you leave you are sure of where you are going.

Recruitment Agencies in Kenya

At the same time, the CS told the 420 recruitment agencies to adhere to the licensing requirements by the vetting board or risk their licences being revoked.

Labour migration is a source of employment and livelihood for migrant workers, and about 97,000 Kenyans work in Saudi Arabia.

He said the government had taken a few initiatives to address some of the challenges Kenyans face in the Gulf state, including renegotiated bilateral agreements.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Faith Murunga and Lorna Jerop escaped jaws of death by a whisker while working as house helps in Saudi.

Faith revealed that her journey to Saudi started with her training in Syokimau, Machakos, on how they were going work there.

However, after jetting out, her passport was taken away on arrival, and things went south.

"The next morning things became bad. Nilianza kuambiwa niwaekee dawa kwa private parts ( I was told to put some medicine on their private parts)," she said.
