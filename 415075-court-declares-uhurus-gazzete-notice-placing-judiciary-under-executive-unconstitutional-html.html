Court Declares Uhuru's Gazette Notice Placing Judiciary Under Executive Unconstitutional

August 2024 · 4 minute read

- The controversial order was issued by President Uhuru Kenyatta in January 2020 and revised in May 2020

- High Court judge James Makau ruled the petitioner will bear the full costs of the petition

- Then chief justice David Maraga said it was wrong to include the Judiciary in the order because it does not fall in any of the categories

The High Court has today, Thursday, June 10, declared President Uhuru Kenyatta's notice aimed at including the Judicial Service Commission and Judiciary under the Executive unconstitutional.

Controversial order

The controversial order, which was issued in January 2020 and revised in May 2020, captured several changes, including the Cabinet secretaries who were either sacked or had their dockets changed.

It also purported to organise the government and set out Judiciary and its Tribunals, Commissions and independent offices as institutions under the functions of ministries and government departments and other constitutional bodies.

"An order of Judicial Review by way of an Order of CERTIORARI be and is hereby granted under Article 23 (3) (f) to remove into the court for purposes of quashing portions of the Executive Order Number 1 of 2020 issued on January 14, 2020 (Revised) May 2020," judge James Aaron Makau ruled.

Makau also ruled that the petitioner will bear the full costs of the petition.

National Treasury

Head of Public Service Joseph Kinyua, the second respondent in the matter, had written to various government departments informing them of the Executive Order and further directing the National Treasury to note the various changes introduced by the order.

"Given the aforesaid whether the second respondent was properly joined or not do, in my view, defeat the petition nor is it fatal. I find no basis on this issue. I dismiss the same," said Makau.

Independent arm of government

In June 2020, then chief justice David Maraga said the Constitution only authorises the president to “direct and coordinate ministries and government departments”.

He said it was wrong to include the Judiciary in the order because it does not fall in any of the above categories as it is an independent arm of government.

“The Judiciary and the Judicial Service Commission are established under Chapter 10 of the Constitution. The order cannot restructure or assign functions to the other co-equal and co-substantial arms of government and independent commissions,” said Maraga.

He said the order had caused a lot of confusion among the public by implying that the Judiciary was under the head of state and was hoping a correction will be issued.

But in a quick rejoinder, Attorney General Kariuki Kihara clarified that the Judiciary was not restructured in the Executive Order as claimed by Maraga.

The AG said no order interfered with the Judiciary's autonomy or any other independent institution in the government.

Uhuru's impeachment

Following the High Court ruling, Law Society of Kenya (LSK) President Nelson Havi said that the move was another ground for Uhuru's impeachment.

This comes after a Nakuru surgeon filed a petition to have Uhuru appoint six judges he left out in his latest appointment.

Magare Gikenyi wanted the courts to declare the rejected judges duly appointed after successful interviews with the Judicial Service Commission (JSC).

According to Magare, Uhuru's action to leave out the six judges contravened the law, and he wanted the entire process quashed.
