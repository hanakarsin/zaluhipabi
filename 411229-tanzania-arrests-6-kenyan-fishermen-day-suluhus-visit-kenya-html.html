Tanzania Arrests 6 Kenyan Fishermen Day after Suluhu's Visit to Kenya

July 2024 · 3 minute read

- The Kenyan fishermen were arrested for encroaching and casting their nets wide into Tanzanian waters

- The arresting officers demanded KSh 50,000 from each of the fishermen in order to set them free

- The arrest came barely a day after the bilateral talk between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Tanzania President Samia Suluhu were held in Nairobi

Tanzanian authorities have arrested six Kenyan nationals who were found fishing in waters considered to be under Tanzania's territory.

The arrest of the six came just a day after President Samia Suluhu visited Kenya for a peacemaking treaty to end a frosty relationship between the two countries.

The fishermen from Kibro Beach in Muhuru Bay, Migori county, were rounded up by Tanzanian police officers who took them to Sota Beach in Northern Mara District.

According to a report filed by Nation, Kibro Beach Management Unit (BMU), Chairman Maulid Joel, the authorities demanded KSh 50,000 for each boat to be released.

“We sent our representatives to seek the release of the two boats and the fishermen, and they were told they have to pay. The boats had the day’s catch and other fishing gear,” Maulid said.

The BMU chair said he shocked that the arrests came when the Tanzanian president was in the country for bilateral talks to strengthen diplomatic ties between the two countries, which had deteriorated during the reign of former president John Pombe Magufuli.

“The heightened cases of arrest by Tanzanian security officers are yet to be resolved. We want the government to assure us of our safety while conducting our businesses,” he added.

On his part, Muhuru Bay MCA, Hevron Maira, said the arrests were an insult to the bilateral talks and peace efforts between the two countries.

“We are also tax-paying Kenyans, and the common wananchi should feel the talks by the two presidents, especially we who live along the Kenya-Tanzania border,” Maira said.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Uhuru had given Tanzanian investors the green light to operate in Kenya without visas and work permits.

Uhuru, while speaking at Serena Hotel, Nairobi, on Wednesday, May 5, during a private sector associations address, urged the investors to follow the law.

"Our investors from Tanzanian should now know that they have the freedom to bring their products and do their businesses in Kenya without their business visas and work permits, but they should follow the laid down laws and protocols," Uhuru said.
