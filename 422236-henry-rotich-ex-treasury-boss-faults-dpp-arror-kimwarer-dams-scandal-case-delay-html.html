..Former Treasury Cabinet Secretary Henry Rotich has taken issue with the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Noordin Haji over what he termed as delay in the case surrounding the controversial Arror and Kimwarer dams scandal.

Through his lawyer, Katwa Kigen, Rotich opposed the request by Haji to be given more time accusing the State of delaying the matter, which was filed in court in 2018.

Kigen asked court to fix matter for hearing

Kigen told the court to fix the matter for hearing, and if there are any amendments to be made by the prosecution, then be done before the hearing date.

Milimani Law Courts Senior Principal Magistrate Felix Kombo gave the prosecution until September 29, 2021, to come up with a proper decision on whom to prosecute and drop from the case.

Haji had asked the court for more time to review charges in the dams scandal case.

Rotich charged afresh

In May 2021, Rotich was charged afresh with four accused people in the Kimwarer and Arror multi-billion shillings scandal.

The two dams were to be constructed in Elgeyo Marakwet county.

The former Treasury boss denied all the charges, conspiracy to defraud, abuse of office, willful failure to comply with applicable laws and engaging in a project without prior planning.

Haji previously said the evidence in the case remains the same, and they will not be introducing new documents, nor shall they be introducing new witnesses.

Rotich and his four co-accused persons appeared before Milimani Anti-corruption Chief Magistrate Douglas Ogoti on Wednesday, May 26, where they denied all the 15 charges.

After a new charge sheet was presented to the court following the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) amending the old one.

The prosecution removed former Principal Secretaries Kamau Thugge and Susan Koech, who had been charged alongside Rotich.

Kamau and Koech will now testify against Rotich during the hearing.

The new charges were read to the accused persons after the defence lawyers Kigen and Phillip Nyachoti, who represented Rotich, did not oppose the DPP move to separate the cases.

David Kiprop among those charged

In this case, former Kerio Valley Development Authority (KVDA) managing director David Kimosop and eight others were charged with corruption in the over KSh 50 billion Arror and Kimwarer multi-purpose dams scandal.

They denied several counts ranging from abuse of office to engaging in unplanned projects.

The DPP has also removed the names of 18 Italians who are still at large from the charge sheet, and they will be charged separately.

The 18 are the directors of Italian construction company CMC di Ravenna who have a separate charge sheet and separate file from Rotich and Kimosop and others after the DPP separated the same so that they may not withhold the proceedings in the current cases before the court.
