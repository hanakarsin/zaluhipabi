Lake Victoria Boat Tragedy: Death Toll Hits 7 as Bad Weather Hinders Rescue Mission

June 2024 Â· 3 minute read
  • As of Wednesday, September 23, seven bodies had been retrieved from the lake
  • Four people are still not accounted for following the Tuesday, September 22 accident
  • County disaster management officer Eluid Onyango ruled out overloading as the cause of the tragedy

The death toll of the boat that capsized in the waters of Lake Victoria one day ago has hit seven.

As of Wednesday, September 22, seven bodies had been retrieved from the lake in Homa Bay county.

However, four people have not been accounted for as they are still in the water body.

Rescue mission ongoing

On Wednesday, five bodies were retrieved in a joint rescue effort coordinated by the Kenya Coast Guard Service, Kenya Red Cross, Kenya Maritime Authority, and the Beach Management Unit.

Two bodies were retrieved on Tuesday, September 21.

In a report by the Daily Nation, among the eight people who were injured, two were still hospitalised while another two received first aid and were released to go home.

The survivors claimed the boat was overloaded and they had expressed concern about it before it set off to sail across the lake.

County disaster management officer Eluid Onyango, however, said the journey had encountered bad weather.

Onyango ruled out the cause of the accident as overloading of passengers and items as claimed by survivors and residents.

"It is very difficult to determine the cause of the boat accident. The boat was seaworthy, and with the bad weather, water started seeping into the boat. The commotion and panic made the boat capsize," he said.

On his part, Kenya Maritime Authority Kisumu regional branch inspector Jeremiah Onyango blamed boat operators for laxity regarding life jackets.

"This is the gospel we have sang as KMA since time immemorial. Passengers should put on life jackets while on water vessels," he said.

Onyango also noted that boat crews tend to take training on vessel safety for granted.

"Just two weeks after they are trained, they operate them without life-saving jackets. As KMA we have not relaxed in ensuring marine safety and reducing boat incidents on the lake," he stated.

Rescue efforts are still ongoing despite the bad weather being experienced in the area.

The tragedy day

The said boat capsized on the evening of Tuesday, September 21, after it left Pier beach at around 4pm for Ndhuru within Mbita sub-county with 16 passengers on board.

According to the Homa Bay Town location senior chief Joshua Ochogo, the boat was carrying excess load, including cement, iron sheets, a number of boxes and some timber at the time of the incident.
