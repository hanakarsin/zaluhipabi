Kenya records highest COVID-19 positive cases in a day in 2021

The CS reported the infections brought to 110,356 the total confirmed positive cases in the country as of March 10

The Ministry of Health has confirmed 713 new coronavirus cases, which is the highest number registered so far in 2021.

In his media address on Wednesday, March 10, Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe said the new infections were detected from a sample size of 5,230 which were tested in the last 24 hours.

Kagwe reported the infections brought to 110,356 the total confirmed positive cases in the country as of March 10, the highest since November 2020.

So far, the country has tested 1, 345, 887 people since the first coronavirus case was reported in Kenya in March 2020.

Deaths and recoveries

At the same time, the disease claimed 12 more lives over the same period increasing the total fatalities to 1,898.

At least 167 patients got discharged from various isolation and care facilities pushing the total number of recoveries to 87,903.

One hundred and fifteen (115) recoveries came from the home-based care and isolation units while another 52 got discharged from hospitals.

The CS also confirmed 563 patients were currently admitted in hospitals out of whom 89 were admitted to ICU.

Vaccination

The government dispelled fears the COVID-19 vaccines may not be safe stressing that only vaccines that had been approved by the World Health Organisation would enter the country.

Upon entry, the vaccines would further be approved by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board before being released for use.

Kenya plans to vaccinate about 15 million people by the end of June 2021.

A special county COVID-19 Vaccine Steering Committee will work closely with the Country COVID-19 deployment and vaccination task force and the sub-county committee.

"I urge all Kenyans to be calm and follow all the guidelines we shall be issuing with regard to the vaccines," Kagwe stressed.
