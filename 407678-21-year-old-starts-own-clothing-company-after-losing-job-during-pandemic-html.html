21-Year-Old Starts Own Clothing Company after Losing Job during Pandemic

July 2024 · 3 minute read

- A young man who lost his job during the COVID-19 pandemic decided to start his own business

- The 21-year-old was previously working at a clothing retail store, and he started his own men's fitting clothing store

- He said he wanted to inspire people who were forced to stay at home rather than dress up and leave the house

A 21-year-o;d young man identified as Evan Harris has inspired many after he officially opened his own men's clothing store, months after losing his job due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The young entrepreneur who lives in Trenton had been working at a suits store until COVID-19 hit, and the clothing store was forced to shut down.

However, rather than sulking due to the experience, he reinvented himself and came up with the idea that he actualised on Monday, March 29.

Evans opened his own distinctive fitting men's suits store and named it BeSuited. The opening ceremony was attended by friends, family and community, including the area's mayor Reed Gusciora,

While interviewing with local media in the area, Evans said:

“I just want to basically get people my own taste of suit-wearing and kind of bring back the suit-wearing. With COVID-19 and everything, it is constantly bringing people inside and limiting people dressing up in general and going out.”

This was precisely why the young businessman came up with unique ways of making people buy new suits.

First, with his mother's help, he managed to get his store opened at a strategic location that once hosted a popular retailing suits shop.

Secondly, he also asked his uncle, who previously worked at the popular retail clothing store for 20 years, to join him and give him insights into the suits business.

Evans said he wanted his shop to be both motivational and transformational to all the men living in his community.

The BeSuited store will offer custom-made suits according to the client's preference. The menswear store has amazing different types of fabrics that come in different colours, pattern, styles and accessories.

While Evans may be a young person, his business is likely to create employment opportunity for lots of people in the future. He is currently working with his mother, sister and uncle.

And he is proof that one can use a bad experience to get a new positive chapter in life.

“I ended up finding a new business that needed renovation and I took the step,” he added during the interview.

His new clothing store will cater to all clients in need of well-fitting clothes and have an on-site tailor present. They will also provide one-on-one consultation for people on dressing matters.
