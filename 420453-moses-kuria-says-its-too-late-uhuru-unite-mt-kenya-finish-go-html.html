Moses Kuria Says It's Too Late for Uhuru to Unite Mt Kenya: &quot;Finish and Go&quot;

  • Deputy President William Ruto is among the 2022 presidential aspirants salivating for Mt Kenya votes
  • Uhuru and Raila are set to begin nationwide tours to counter Ruto's political wave ahead of the 2022 General Election

Outspoken Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria has urged President Uhuru Kenyatta to finish his term in office and leave, saying that it is late for him to unite the Mt Kenya region.

Uhuru and Raila's magical handshake

Kuria accused Uhuru of neglecting the vote-rich region in his development agenda for the nation following his magical handshake with Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga in March 2018.

The lawmaker who does not shy away from controversy claimed that their own government had abandoned the Mt Kenya region residents.

''For the past four years, we have been lamenting that our businesses have collapsed. We have been complaining that enforcement officers are harassing traders in Nyamakima and Gilomba. Still, nothing has been done to that effect,'' Kuria said during a burial ceremony in the region.

According to Kuria, who is allied to the Hustler Nation associated with Deputy President William Ruto, most public medical facilities in the region lack drugs, while some areas are without proper road infrastructure.

''We still have some places in Mt Kenya where there is no running tap water. Yet, despite raising these concerns, some of us have been labelled as madmen. Since the General Election is just around the corner, some people are busy calling for us to unite with them, but I want to tell them that this cannot happen because it is late,'' Kuria said.

The GEMA community

Kuria said that the GEMA community could have embraced the unity if those calling for unity reached up to them earlier.

''You reached to us when it was late. If you wanted to unite with us, you could have come much earlier. We overwhelmingly voted in this government to the last man, but instead of providing us with essential services, they resorted to singing to us reggae when we are suffering. So we are telling you, finish and go,'' Kuria explained.

Uhuru, Raila planned nationwide tours

Kuria's remarks come when Uhuru and Raila are set to begin nationwide tours to counter Ruto's ahead of the 2022 polls.

The tours will start in Nyanza, Western, Coast and Upper Eastern, where several projects will be commissioned.

Raila confirmed the tours during his Murang'a visit on Tuesday, July 27.

"Next week we shall be in Nyanza then move to Western and coast. We shall criss-cross the entire country to unite Kenyans as one nation with Uhuru standing on one side and Raila the other," Raila said.

The tours would come ahead of the highly anticipated Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) judgment by the Court of Appeal set for August 20.
