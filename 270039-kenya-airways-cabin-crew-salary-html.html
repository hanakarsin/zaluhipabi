Kenya Airways cabin crew salary

If the perfectly well-clad cabin crew members don’t flatter you, then you definitely love their interpersonal interaction with clients aboard commercial aircraft. Kenya Airways offers opportunities to qualified and young Kenyans every year to serve in the position of cabin attendants. The airline has an established recruiting process and their wages are relatively completive compared to other regional airlines. Here’s all you need to know about cabin crew salary Kenya Airways.

About the airline

Kenya Airways has been Kenya’s national carrier since 1977, after the fall of the East African Community. Its operations are based in its main hub – The Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA). The airline has grown tremendously to become the continent’s leading airline. As a result, the airline has managed to earn lots of accolades, including the annual World Travel Awards for offering excellent business class services in Africa for a commendable five consecutive years.

During the many years, it has been in operation, it has grown in leaps and bounds to accumulate an inventory of over 40 commercial airliners currently serving different destinations in Africa, Europe, Middle East and lately the United States.

The Kenyan national carrier is assigned an international code; KQ by the International Air Transport Association (IATA); tasked with the role of overseeing safe and secure aviation industry in the world. It has been a member of the Sky Team alliance since 2010.

It owns a subsidiary company that it operates as a budget regional airline.

Kenya Airways air hostess requirements

Being a cabin crew requires more than just your academic credentials. You will have to meet these Kenya Airways air hostess requirement standards to pass the checklist to your dream job. They are highlighted below.

  • Have a KCSE mean Grade of C-(minus).
  • Be 21 years and above.
  • Meet the certain physical height of 5.4 ft. and 5.6 ft. for ladies and gentlemen respectively.
  • Ability to swim.
  • Exemplary interpersonal and teamwork skills.
  • IT proficient.
  • Experience in customer care service is recommended.
  • Ability to work comfortably in a multicultural environment.
  • High level of personal grooming and eloquence.
  • Mentally and physically fit to meet aircrew standards.

A large percentage of Kenya Airways cabin crews are recruited from its premier training facility, where in-house training takes place. The state-of-the-art KQ Pride center situated within JKIA offers high-quality training to cabin crews and other aviation trainees in Cargo operations, aviation security etc. Pertaining to the standard of training, the center is accredited by IATA.

To earn a certificate from the institution you will be required to undergo 3 months of training in emergency theory, on-board Cabin Services, and Basic Life Support. The whole program will cost you Kshs.155,000. Here's what's expected of a KQ cabin crew member.

  • Cabin crew are expected to uphold the highest standards and ethics irrespective of the job description that encompasses the below duties and responsibilities
  • Offer in-flight services like preparing and serving meals and refreshments.
  • Provide safety and emergency instruction to the passengers throughout the flight. Handling of safety procedures in conjunction with the flight crew in emergency cases is one of the priority tasks.
  • Attending to the passenger inquiries and complaints.

The salary for Kenya Airways cabin crew

It is no doubt Kenya Airways offer the best opportunities to various aviation professional to join the prestigious airline. The Kenya Airways staff benefits and salaries are competitive compared to other airlines. Moreover, the allowances and benefits you are ought to be awarded once you join the ranks of its workforce are immense. The Airline offers on-job training, flexible and convenient working terms.

The basic salary and allowances you will be paid as a cabin crew depends on the level of expertise and other accrued benefits depending on the work station. Generally, the salary ranges between Kshs.50,000 - Kshs.90,000. When combined with the allowances, the amount increases to to roughly Kshs.120,000.

Overall, Kenya Airways employees salaries may not be as high as the other high-class world airlines, but the staff enjoys one of the country’s best working conditions. This is, of course, is in terms of the earnings, job security and career advancement. So if you get the opportunity to be recruited as the airline's cabin crew, be assured you won’t be getting the short end of the stick.
