The Kenya Metrological department has asked residents of 15 counties to prepare for strong winds likely to cause massive destruction of their homes and the surrounding environment.

The winds will be accompanied by large waves at the coastal area and are expected to strike on Thursday, July 16, and persist for two more days.

According to the weatherman, the gale will be moving at a speed of 30 knots (15.4 metres/second) on Thursday and strengthen to 40 knots (20.56 m/s) on Friday, July 17.

On Saturday, July 18, the winds will be expected to reduce to 30 knots (15.4 m/s).

"The wave height is likely to be three metres over the Kenya coastal waters on Friday and is expected to reduce to 2.5 metres on Sunday, July 19," said the met depart.

Counties likely to be affected by the strong winds include Marsabit, Turkana, Samburu, Mandera, Wajir, Isiolo and Garissa.

Others are Kitui, Makueni, Tana River, Taita Taveta, Lamu, Kilifi, Mombasa and Kwale.

The department asked residents in the above-mentioned counties to be on the lookout for potentially destructive winds expected to strike starting Thursday.

"Strong winds may blow off roofs, uproot trees and cause structural damages. Very high waves may lower visibility, cause capsizing of boats and threaten the safety of those engaged in water sports and recreational activities. Those in the areas of concern should be on high alert," warned the met department.

In October 2019, strong winds that blew off roofs and reduced visibility hit several counties in the country among them Nairobi and Mombasa.

Motorists were cautioned against driving into or through the dust storm while those in the dust already were advised to turn their headlights, slow down and safely pull over.

The department also advised people with asthmatic conditions and respiratory-related diseases to stay indoors.
