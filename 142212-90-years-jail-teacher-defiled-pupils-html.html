Teacher in Murang'a sentenced to 90 years in jail

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- A teacher in Murang'a is likely to spend the rest of his life in jail after a court handed him 90 years behind bars

- John Gichia was found guilty of sexually assaulting 10 pupils of a school he was teaching

- Gichia is said to have molested the pupils in their dormitory at night

- The pupils told the court that he would fondle their private parts and kiss them

A court in Murang’a county handed a primary school teacher 90 years in jail for sexually assaulting 10 pupils.

John Gichia Mugi, 23, was found guilty of sexually assaulting the 10 pupils of Muthiria Primary School in Kiharu constituency between January and May, 2015.

The sentence came after the victims testified how the disgraced teacher would lure them, kiss them and fondle their private parts.

The victims are aged between 13 and 16.

Resident Magistrate Jesse Masiga said the teacher would serve nine years for each count, amounting to 90 years in jail.

Gichia was accused by the said pupils when one of them hinted to his parents who had visited that they were being molested by the teacher.

The parents reported the matter to the school principal who, however, tried to cover up. He would later be found guilty of professional misconduct and was discontinuity by the Teachers Service Commission (TSC).

The victims also told the court that the said teacher tried to molest them in their dormitories but they resisted him.

Defending himself, Gichia denied the accusations saying the pupils had been influenced by three teachers who were jealousy of him after he was promoted to be head of the science department.

However, the magistrate dismissed him saying the children had a good relationship with him and it takes a lot of courage to report sexual molestation.

The magistrate said he believes the pupils’ accounts were true.

In 2015, a Sunday school teacher was arrested by police following the revelation that he had molested at least nine children under his care.

David Mugo, a Sunday school teacher at Kasarani Presbyterian Church of East Africa (PCEA), was alleged to have committed many of the acts during the teachers strike of September 2015.

The father of one of the children saw his son acting strangely around Mugo and questioned him about it. Upon learning of the alleged abuse, he called the police.

The Sunday school teacher would commonly drug the young children at his home and have sexual relations with them. He then threatened them so they could not speak about the acts.
