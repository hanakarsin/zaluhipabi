John DeMathews 2 Widows Dedicate Lovely Song to Late Musician during His Memorial

May 2024 · 3 minute read
  • John DeMathew’s widows Carolyne and Sarafina recently celebrated their late husband's second death anniversary
  • During the occasion held at a Nairobi club, the two dedicated a song to the late, noting it was meant to honour him and not for monetary gain
  • The performance was, however, cut short as one of the widows was overcome with emotions

Late Mugithi singer John DeMathew’s widows Sarafina and Caroline are more than just co-wives. They are also best of friends.

The two continue to carry their late husband's memory and celebrate him since his tragic passing in 2019 due to a road accident.

As the late singer's fans, friends and family marked his second death anniversary at the Delview Annex Lounge on Saturday, August 14, the duo dedicated a beautiful song to him.

Taking to the stage, Carolyne and Sarafina, all dressed in white, explained the reason behind composing the song.

Carolyne said she was not doing it for fame or money but to celebrate her late husband, adding she had seen some criticising them for composing it.

"I was doing it for my husband, not for fame or money. I'm not being rude, because I've seen some messages claiming I can't sing. After all I already have the fame but I was doing it to celebrate my husband," she said.

Holding each other close, the two started their performance with a bit of dance before Carolyne started her verse, singing about how much their love was genuine and thanking God for bringing them together.

Towards the end of the performance, Carolyne broke into tears as she was overcome with emotions and those on stage, including her co-wife, gave her a shoulder to lean on.
