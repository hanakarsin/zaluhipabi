Court order suspending acting CJ Mwilu is act of judicial terror, Orengo

- Meru court issued orders barring Mwilu from acting as the chief justice following retirement of David Maraga upon attaining 70 years of age

- The judge also restrained the deputy chief justice from sitting in the Judicial Service Commission

- Senior counsel said the ruling will have far reaching implications key among among will be breeding a judicial anarchy

Siaya senator and senior counsel James Orengo has sharply differed with Meru judge Patrick Otieno following restraining orders against Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu.

The High Court judge had on Friday, January 29, issued orders barring Mwilu from acting as the chief justice following retirement of David Maraga upon attaining 70 years of age.

According to Orengo who is also the Senate minority leader, Otieno's ruling will have far reaching implications key among among is breeding a judicial anarchy.

"The rule of law is broken and judicial anarchy sets in when judicial processes are used and abused to achieve unlawful outcomes or brazen political objectives. The orders obtained ex parte against CJ Mwilu (Ag) is an egregious act of judicial terror and oppression," he stated.

The ruling will be in effect until a case filed by petitioner Isaiah Mwongela is determined.

Otieno also restrained the deputy chief justice from sitting in the Judicial Service Commission.

Mischief

Soon after the judgment, Law Society of Kenya president Nelson Havi who read mischief in the decision claiming it could have been influenced by external political forces keen to block and frustrate Mwilu.

"A judge cannot issue an injunction restraining another Judge from acting. Besides, Justice Mrima dismissed a similar case. Only two explanations for this: deep State has a lot of dirt on Justice P J Otieno and have banked a post-dated cheque given to it or the order is a fraud," he stated.
