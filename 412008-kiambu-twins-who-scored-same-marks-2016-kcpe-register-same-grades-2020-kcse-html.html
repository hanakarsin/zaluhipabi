Kiambu Twins Who Scored Same Marks in 2016 KCPE Register Same Grades in 2020 KCSE

August 2024 · 3 minute read

- Victor Kuria and Vincent Kahari attained similar grades in the 2020 KCSE

- Four years ago, they scored similar marks of 394 in the 2016 KCPE examinations

- The two have been indistinguishable since birth, and they both aspire to be medical doctors

Identical twins Victor Kuria and Vincent Kahari from Kiambu county share more than just looks.

The two, who scored the same marks in the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) four years ago, coincidentally attained similar grades in the recently released 2020 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) results.

Victor and Vincent amazed many in 2016 when they both scored 394 marks in KCPE before joining the same secondary school a month later, NTV reported.

The two boys pulled another surprise after they scored grade A- with 75 and 76 points in the KCSE results released on Monday, May 10.

They attributed their success to healthy competition, which aimed to better each other to get good grades.

"We used to have healthy competition and continued supporting each other, although we never got the same grades after KCPE until this one," they said

The two said they would be pursuing medicine as they both aspire to become doctors.

According to their father, James Gathungu, he expected a good grade from the boys, but he never imagined that they would score grade A-(minus).

"I was expecting the boys to get B+, I never imaged that they would score an A- , but I must say that I am very impressed by the performance and there teachers are also happy,"

Their mother, Lucy Njeri, said that the twins care of each other and are hardly separated.
