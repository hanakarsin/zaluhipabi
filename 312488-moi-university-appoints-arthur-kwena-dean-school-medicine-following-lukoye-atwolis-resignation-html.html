Moi University appoints Arthur Kwena dean school of medicine following Lukoye Atwoli's resignation

February 2024 · 2 minute read

- Lukoye Atwoli resigned from his position as a dean on Thursday, August 8

- He cited frustrations by colleagues and the university's management as reason for his exit

- Barely 24 hours later, the institution appointed Arthur Kwena to replace Atwoli

Moi University administration has appointed Arthur Kwena as new dean for school of medicine following the unanticipated resignation of Lukoye Atwoli.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, Atwoli resigned from the position on Thursday, August 8, citing frustrations by colleagues and the university's management.

The university also appointed Robert Tenga Kuremu as principal in the college of health sciences in acting capacity.

Kwena is an associate professor of medical biochemistry.

"Professor Kwena brings transformational leadership and vast experience that will see the school move to greater heights" said the university administration.

Following Atwoli's exit, students called on the administrators to intervene and solve the stalemate, with some fearing they would miss out on their August 2019 graduation.

The students hailed Atwoli as having been in the forefront in enhancing quality education and smooth running of activities in the school.

Student leader Anthony Wamae disclosed they were supposed to graduate in 2018 but strikes led to lack of quorums to deliberate results that made it impossible.

For the past two years, the institution's learning calendar has been affected by six strikes involving lecturers, doctors and nurses.

Notably, when medics are on strike, it means there are no ward rotations because the students learn from patients’ admissions.
