Odinga Familys Hand in Mt Kenya Region's Grip on Power

Opposition leader Raila Odinga has been portrayed as an enemy of the people of M Kenya for a long time, with critics persistently pushing the narrative that he is unsellable in the vote-rich region.

However, as the 2022 General Election approaches, things appear to be changing so fast in favour of Raila’s political resuscitation in Mt Kenya.

The people of the mountain appear to be slowly acknowledging and appreciating what Baba has done for the region for the past four decades he has been in politics.

The son of Jaramogi is subtly, yet crucially, winning over voters in Mt Kenya, sending chills down the spine of his opponents as he has not enjoyed any support there during his four previous attempts at the presidency.

Equipped with his wit and rich knowledge of Kenya’s history, Raila has seized the opportunity presented by the handshake to engage Kikuyus and make it clear that he is the leader they want, a vanguard of democracy and beacon of hope.

His detractors have been wondering how to counter the growing admiration of a man whose name they have for years soaked in tonnes of venomous propaganda to scare away the community.

Oginga Odinga sacrifice

Yet, Raila’s admiration is not without basis. The Odinga family has greatly helped the mountain politically since independence. It has made sacrifices for the region to extents not demonstrated by any other political family.

At the onset of Kenya’s self-rule in 1963, Raila’s father, Jaramogi Oginga Odinga, refused to be sworn in as the country’s prime minister until Jomo Kenyatta, a Kikuyu, was released from custody.

Jaramogi held Mzee Kenyatta in high regard and never contemplated taking over power while he was still in prison and coined the slogan “Uhuru na Kenyatta”, forcing colonial authorities to set him free.

It was a demonstration of unparalleled love, sacrifice and patriotism.

The gesture has remained alive in the minds of many Mt Kenya natives despite the negative publicity that has been sold to them for years.

Raila carries father's legacy

Raila has carried on with his father's legacy, crucially lending a political hand to the region without asking for favours in return.

In 2002, when the country was yearning for a change of guard from the oppressive KANU regime that had been in power for 24 years, Raila made a personal sacrifice that changed the dynamics of Kenya’s politics for good.

Jakom pulled a fast one on KANU when he joined the independence party but jumped ship shortly after and endorsed Mwai Kibaki’s candidature, in what has now been engraved in history books as the ‘Kibaki Tosha’ moment.

The victory was momentous, especially for the Kikuyu community, which had suffered political persecution under the KANU regime.

Mt Kenya region strived in vain to wrestle power from Daniel Moi since the early 1980s until Raila’s sacrifice happened in 2002.

By endorsing Kibaki, Raila ostensibly laid the ground for another Kikuyu, Uhuru Kenyatta, to ascend to power in 2013.

Although the ODM leader has had friction with a section of Mt. Kenya political heavyweights, he has always mended walls and supported their cause.

For instance, in 2007, when it was almost apparent that the system had rigged him out, Raila swallowed his pride and shook hands with Kibaki to end the violence that had rocked the country.

Despite accepting to share power with Kibaki, a Kikuyu, he still appointed a host of Mt Kenya leaders to top government positions. Among them was Joe Nyagah, who he appointed minister for Cooperative Development.

Peace gesture

In 2013, the former premier was unsatisfied with the outcome of the presidential poll and challenged it at the Supreme Court.

Although he did not agree with the court’s ruling, Raila allowed Uhuru’s Jubilee administration to take power as he honourably took over the opposition role.

Fast forward to 2018, and Jakom pulled another surprise when he stepped out with Uhuru and shook hands on the floor of Harambee House to mark the end of their divisive 2017 political feud.

It was a tough decision for Raila, coming hot on the heels of the acrimonious 2017 presidential election, whose results the Supreme Court annulled.

The peace gesture shocked even Raila’s allies and strategists, who had launched a countrywide Resist Campaign to boycott products from certain firms, mostly those with Mt Kenya ownership.

It would not have been easy for Uhuru to run the country without the handshake. Protests and weekly demonstrations became unbearable, businesses were shut, and the country’s economy shrank drastically.

Being a true statesman, Raila, like he did with Kibaki, chose peace over violence, dialogue over sideshows, and nationalism over personal interests. He shook hands with Uhuru, contrary to the expectations of millions of Kenyans.

When Deputy President William Ruto defected from the government and left Uhuru to carry the burden of Jubilee legacy alone, the former premier came through and stood with him through tumultuous moments that included handling the Covid-19 pandemic and resuscitating the economy.

In parliament, ODM MPs have been at the forefront championing and voting for all government policies to enable the president to run the country effectively and achieve his legacy agenda.

Mt Kenya benefit

Raila has been instrumental in driving the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) amendment bill to create inclusivity in leadership and equality in resource allocation.

Standing with Uhuru in pushing this agenda, political analysts argue, is likely to benefit Mt Kenya more than his Nyanza backyard.

All these reasons perhaps explain why Mt Kenya residents have started to have a different view of who really Raila is.

The perception that Baba is their worst enemy is dying down, and political heavyweights in the region have started to prepare the ground for the former premier ahead of 2022.

On Tuesday, July 27, five governors and over 50 political leaders from the vote-rich region threw weight behind Raila’s presidential ambitions.

Former Gatanga MP Peter Kenneth clarified that the Mt Kenya region had nothing against Raila and attributed the past political rivalry to politics.

“Whatever was said of Raila was as a result of political competition and nothing else. We have gone past that and we now want to focus on progressive politics. As a region we want to invite you (Raila) to visit this place frequently. you can come and sleep at my place, attend a church service and move around so that people can see you,” said Kenneth.

Kirinyaga governor Anne Waiguru and her Murang’a counterpart Mwangi was Iria also invited Raila to traverse Mt Kenya region frequently.

Kiambu governor James Nyoro hinted that a lineup of political heavyweights had been constituted to drum up support for the former prime minister.

Other Mt Kenya top guns understood to be rallying behind Raila's presidential bid include Laikipia governor Nderitu Mureithi, Agriculture CS Peter Munya, Jubilee vice chair David Murathe, ex-Kiambu governor William Kabogo, Kieni MP Kanini Kega, Nyeri Town’s Wambugu Ngunjiri and nominated MP Maina Kamanda among others.

Raila's Mt Kenya star

Political analyst Mark Bichachi opines that Raila’s political star in Mt Kenya has started to shine and will soon eclipse Ruto’s popularity bubble.

“The messaging and approach Raila is using is working for him and will soon turn the tables in Mt Kenya, which has been under the temporary grip of Tanga Tanga. From the events of the Music concert in Ndakaini last week, one can easily project the political capital and manpower Raila is going to get from the mountain,” noted Bichachi.

Political analyst Herman Manyora argues that every vote Raila secures from Mt Kenya will be good for his presidential bid.

He says that if Raila garners more than 30% of the Mt Kenya votes, he will most likely romp to State House without struggling.

“For Raila, anything above 30% is okay for him, but for William Ruto, he will have to pull not less than 80% of the mountain votes. When you look at those figures then you can easily tell who is likely to achieve his targets without struggling. From where I sit, Raila is likely to get more than 30% of Mt Kenya votes,” opined Manyora.
