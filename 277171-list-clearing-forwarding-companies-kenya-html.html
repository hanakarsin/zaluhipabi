Clearing and forwarding procedures in Kenya

With a list of clearing and forwarding companies you can trust, it helps if you know what the process of clearing your goods entails. This may be useful especially when you are worried about the duration it is taking. You will manage your expectations and gauge whether the clearing of your goods is taking longer than necessary. Here are the notable procedures for clearing your goods in Kenya:

  • First, an importer has to acquire the services of a clearing agent
  • The clearing agent will process all the paperwork through the KRA online platform
  • You need several documents for your goods to get to the entry point. The basic ones include import declaration form, bill of lading, and certificate of compliance where needed.
  • Make sure your clearing agent is licensed by KRA through their customs department
  • You will need to pay some fees including the CIF and IDF. How much these figures will be is dependent upon the assessment by the customs department who are guided by the value of the consignment as well as the relevant duty rate. The common external tariff forms the basis for duty rates laid on imported items in Kenya and East Africa
  • The local imports for Kenya in containers are moved to different container freight station and a specific vessel as a means of de-congesting the port.
  • Upon proper certification and declaration of your cargo by the ports authority, your clearing agent is free to collect it.
